Question 1: Solution

  1. Econometrics is the science and art of using economic theory and statistical techniques to analyze economic data.

  2. Experimental data is the data collected through experiments designed to evaluate the effect of a treatment or a policy.

  3. Observational data is the data obtained by observing actual behavior outside an experimental setting. This type of data is usually collected through surveys.

  4. The cross-sectional data is the data on different entities—workers, consumers, firms, governmental units, and so forth—for a single time period.

  5. Panel data is the data for multiple entities in which each entity is observed at multiple time periods.

  6. Time series the data is the data for a single entity (person, firm, country) collected at multiple time periods.

  7. An Ideal Randomized Controlled Experiment (IRCE) is a research design where entities or subjects are randomly assigned to treatment and control groups, enabling the assessment of the causal effect of the treatment on a specific outcome.

  8. The causal effect is the effect on an outcome of a given action or treatment, as measured in an ideal randomized controlled experiment (RCE). Alternatively, it is the effect of an action on an outcome when everything else is constant (ceteris paribus effect).

  9. A confounding variable (omitted variable) is a variable that is correlated with both the treatment variable and the outcome variable, but is not included in the analysis.

Question 2

Let \(x_1,\ldots,x_n\) denote a sequence of numbers; \(y_1,\ldots,y_n\) denote another sequence of numbers; and \(a\), \(b\), and \(c\) denote three constants. Show that

  1. \(\sum_{i=1}^n ax_i = a \sum_{i=1}^n x_i\).

  2. \(\sum_{i=1}^n (x_i+y_i) = \sum_{i=1}^n x_i + \sum_{i=1}^n y_i\).

  3. \(\sum_{i=1}^n b = n \times b\).

  4. \(\sum_{i=1}^n (a+bx_i+cy_i)^2 = na^2+b^2\sum_{i=1}^nx^2_i+c^2\sum_{i=1}^ny^2_i+2ab\sum_{i=1}^nx_i+2ac\sum_{i=1}^ny_i+2bc\sum_{i=1}^nx_iy_i\).

Question 2: Solution

\[\begin{align*} \sum_{i=1}^n ax_i &= ax_1 + ax_2 + \cdots + ax_n \\ &= a(x_1 + x_2 + \cdots + x_n) \\ &= a\sum_{i=1}^n x_i. \end{align*}\]

\[\begin{align*} \sum_{i=1}^n (x_i+y_i) &= x_1 + y_1 + x_2 + y_2 + \cdots + x_n + y_n \\ &= (x_1 + x_2 + \cdots + x_n) + (y_1 + y_2 + \cdots + y_n) \\ &= \sum_{i=1}^nx_i+\sum_{i=1}^ny_i. \end{align*}\]

\[\begin{align*} \sum_{i=1}^n b &= \sum_{i=1}^n b x_i^0 \\ &= \underbrace{b + b + \cdots + b}_{n \text{ times}} \\ &= n \times b. \end{align*}\]

\[\begin{align*} \sum_{i=1}^n (a+bx_i+cy_i)^2 &= \sum_{i=1}^n \Big[a^2 + (bx_i)^2 +(cy_i)^2 + 2(abx_i + acy_i + bx_icy_i)\Big] \\ &= \sum_{i=1}^n \Big[a^2 + b^2x_i^2 + c^2y_i^2 + 2abx_i + 2acy_i + 2bx_icy_i\Big] \\ &= \sum_{i=1}^n a^2 + \sum_{i=1}^n b^2x_i^2 + \sum_{i=1}^n c^2y_i^2 + \sum_{i=1}^n 2abx_i + \sum_{i=1}^n 2acy_i + \sum_{i=1}^n 2bx_icy_i \\ &= na^2+b^2\sum_{i=1}^nx^2_i+c^2\sum_{i=1}^ny^2_i+2ab\sum_{i=1}^nx_i+2ac\sum_{i=1}^ny_i+2bc\sum_{i=1}^nx_iy_i. \end{align*}\]

Question 3

Describe a hypothetical ideal randomized controlled experiment to study the effect of hours spent studying on performance in an econometrics midterm exam. Suggest some impediments to implementing this experiment in practice.

Solution to Question 3

The experiment should include one or more treatment groups and a control group. For example, one treatment could require studying for four hours, while the control group would not study at all. Students would be randomly assigned to these groups, and the causal effect of study hours on midterm performance would be estimated by comparing the average midterm grades of the treatment groups to those of the control group. The main challenge is ensuring that students in each group follow the assigned study conditions. How do you ensure that control group students refrain from studying, as this could impact their grades? Similarly, how do you ensure that treatment group students complete the full four hours of study?
